Description

Doro Wat is a rich and spicy Ethiopian chicken stew made with berbere spice, caramelized onions, and spiced butter (niter kibbeh)

It often includes hard-boiled eggs and is served with injera, a sourdough flatbread. This dish is deeply flavorful and a centerpiece of Ethiopian cuisine.

ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 Â½ cups water, divided
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cubed

Steps

  1. step 1: Clean the chicken pieces, rub them with lemon juice, and rinse. Set aside.
  2. step 2: In a heavy pot, cook finely chopped onions over medium heat (no oil) until soft and golden brown. Stir frequently.
  3. step 3: Add the spiced butter and berbere spice to the onions, cooking until fragrant.
  4. step 4: Add the chicken pieces and coat them in the onion-spice mixture. Pour in the broth or water. Simmer until the chicken is tender.
  5. step 5: Add hard-boiled eggs and simmer for another 5-10 minutes to let them absorb the flavors.
